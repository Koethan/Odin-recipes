Ropa Vieja

Ropa Vieja is one of Cuba’s most loved and famous dishes.

This rustic, slow-cooked beef is made in a tomato wine sauce along with some flavorful ingredients and herbs. It tastes out of this world delicious. The recipe is an amazing treat to impress your family and friends with.

Ropa Vieja Ingredients

  • Beef – Chuck roast.
  • Seasonings and Herbs – Cumin, oregano, sazon, adobo, paprika, smoked paprika, garlic powder, cloves, all spice, bay leaves and pepper.
  • Veggies – Onions, bell peppers and tomatoes.
  • Flavor enhancers – Wine, chicken broth, garlic, tomato paste, olives and capers.

Ropa Vieja Steps

  1. Cut the chuck into a few large chunks. Mix cumin, oregano, garlic powder, paprika, sazon, adobo, salt and pepper in a bowl and season the chuck pieces with the mixture generously.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan and sear the meat chunks all over till brown. Plate them out.
  3. In the same pan, sauté onion and bell peppers till soft. Add in garlic along with oregano, cumin, paprika, smoked paprika, clove and allspice powder and sauté for a few minutes.
  4. Pour in wine and simmer for 5 minutes. Add in crushed tomatoes and tomato paste along with chicken broth. Cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add back in the beef chunks along with bay leaves and cook for 3-4 hours, covered, till the meat is fork tender. Stir it every 30-40 minutes.
  6. Remove the meat to a plate and shred with two forks, then stir back into the sauce.
  7. At the end, stir in olives and capers and cook for another 10 minutes. And there you go… Ropa Vieja ready to dig in to.
